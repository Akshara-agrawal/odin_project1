Description:

These classic vanilla cupcakes are soft, moist, and fluffy with a delicate crumb and rich buttery vanilla flavor. They are perfect for birthdays, parties, or as a quick tea-time treat. Topped with creamy buttercream frosting, they look beautiful and taste delicious. This is a foolproof recipe for beginners to bake perfect cupcakes every time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 and 1/4 cups (160g) all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 and 1/4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 cup (113g) unsalted butter, softened (or use salted butter and skip extra salt)
  • 2 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla essence
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) milk (room temperature)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 180¬∞C (350¬∞F). Line a cupcake tray with cupcake liners.
  2. In a bowl, sift flour, baking powder, and salt together. Keep aside.
  3. In another bowl, beat butter and sugar using a hand whisk or electric mixer until it becomes light and fluffy (about 2-3 mins).
  4. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  5. Add vanilla essence and mix.
  6. Now add half of the flour mixture, fold gently, then add milk, and finally add the remaining flour mixture. Mix
  7. just until combined. Do not overmix.
  8. Divide the batter evenly into the cupcake liners (fill about 2/3rd).
  9. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  10. Let them cool completely before frosting.

Tips:

  • Always use room temperature butter and eggs for best results.
  • Do not overmix the batter to keep cupcakes light and fluffy.
  • Decorate with sprinkles, chocolate chips, fruit pieces, or edible glitter for a festive look.
  • For eggless cupcakes, let me know and I will provide a tested recipe.
