Description

Dumplings are small pieces of dough wrapped around savoury fillings like meat or vegetables, then steamed, boiled, or fried. They are popular in many Asian cuisines, especially Chinese, and are enjoyed as appetisers, snacks, or full meals.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • Pinch of salt

For the filling:

  • 250g ground chicken, pork, or beef
  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 2-3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough:
    • In a bowl, mix flour and salt.
    • Add warm water slowly, mixing until a dough forms.
    • Knead for 5-10 minutes until smooth.
    • Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling:
    • In a bowl, combine ground meat, chopped cabbage, green onions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper.
    • Mix well until fully combined.
  3. Make the dumplings:
    • Divide the dough into small pieces and roll each into a thin circle (about 3 inches diameter).
    • Place a teaspoon of filling in the center of each wrapper.
    • Fold in half to make a semi-circle and pinch edges to seal. You can pleat the edges for a traditional look.
  4. Cook the dumplings:
    • To steam: Place in a steamer lined with cabbage leaves or parchment paper and steam for 10-15 minutes.
    • To boil: Drop into boiling water and cook until they float to the top plus 2 minutes more.
    • To pan-fry: Heat oil in a pan, place dumplings flat side down, cook until bottom is golden, add 1/4 cup water, cover and steam until water evaporates.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve hot with soy sauce, chilli oil, or your favourite dipping sauce.

Serving Suggestion

Enjoy dumplings with soy sauce mixed with vinegar and chilli for a traditional dipping experience.
